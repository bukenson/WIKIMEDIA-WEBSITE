About Us

Is a community of students and knowledge enthusiasts dedicated to promoting open knowledge through Wikipedia and its sister projects

Our mission is to empower students with research, writing, and digital skills while contributing to the expansion of free and accessible information about Nigeria, Africa, and the world. Through workshops, edit-a-thons, and collaborative projects, we enhance the visibility of local content, ensuring that our stories, history, and culture are well-represented on global platforms. By joining the OAU Wiki Fan Club, members become part of a movement that values knowledge-sharing, digital literacy, and the power of information to create positive change.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the OAU Wiki Fan Club?

The OAU Wiki Fan Club is a student-led community at Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU), Ile-Ife, dedicated to promoting open knowledge, digital literacy, and Wikipedia contributions. We train students in Wikipedia editing, research, and content creation to help document Nigerian and African narratives.

2. Who can join the OAU Wiki Fan Club?

Any student of Obafemi Awolowo University who is interested in knowledge-sharing, research, writing, and digital skills can join. No prior experience with Wikipedia is required—we provide all the training you need!

3. What do members of the club do?

Members engage in: Wikipedia editing and article creation Workshops and training sessions on Wikipedia and Wikimedia projects Edit-a-thons and campaigns focused on Nigerian and African content Collaborative projects with Wikimedia User Group Nigeria (WUGN)

4. Do I need prior experience to contribute to Wikipedia?

No! Our club offers training sessions for beginners to learn how to edit Wikipedia correctly, follow citation rules, and create quality content.

5. How can I become an active member?

You can become an active member by: Attending club meetings and training sessions Participating in edit-a-thons, outreach programs, and Wikipedia campaigns Contributing articles, edits, and research on Wikipedia

6. What projects has the club worked on?

No, membership is completely free! All you need is a passion for learning and contributing to open knowledge.

7. Are there any membership fees?

The OAU Wiki Fan Club has successfully organized: Wikipedia SDGs Campus Tour 2.0 (OAU Edition) – Focused on bridging content gaps related to SDG 3, 5, and 13 (Good Health, Gender Equality, and Climate Action). Wiki In School Program 2023 – Empowering teachers to train students on Wikipedia editing and 21st-century skills. Wiki for Human Rights 2024 – Expanding Wikipedia content related to human rights and environmental sustainability in Southwest Nigeria. Wikipedia Awareness and Training in OAU – Training students in Wikipedia editing, formatting, and content creation.
